{{ data['recommendationheadings']['why'] }}

For services to be able to share data across government, via the GOV.UK account and without significant manual data processing, services must be sharing data in the same or compatible formats.

Our research found that many services, particularly the less digitally mature, do not have a data dictionary in place, presenting an opportunity to develop a cross-government dictionary for services to adopt as part of their digital transformation roadmap.

{{ data['recommendationheadings']['what'] }}

Developing a data dictionary will enable:

  • Services undergoing a digital transformation to move towards greater conformance and compatibility in the data they record
  • Programmes within GDS to have sight of the data items used for cross-government data sharing, and build them into cross-government features
