What's not in the Phase 2 scope
  • Usability testing of the dashboard and account space as the scope of our work was to understand the value creating an account adds to users in regards to a joined up and personalised journey across GOV.UK, and to consider the design challenges that may come about in the future.

    Detailed UI design and testing of presenting the account to users would duplicate work carried out within the Accounts Team.
  • Explore technical architecture and technical solutions as the Manage Your Data Team are moving in to an Alpha where they are exploring the feasibility of PODS
  • Explore ownership of data as PODS is a solution that allows users to own their data
  • A deep dive in to legislation and governance (however we captured insights that came out of research)

Phase 3

Collaborating with other teams across the programme to share our knowledge and insights, while making suggestions for the next steps

Creating an implementation roadmap for future account features, based on findings from phase 1 and 2

Objectives

1

Communicate recommendations for prioritised features to relevant teams

View details
  • Provide actionable design, data and technical recommendations to the relevant teams required to deliver prioritised features.
  • Allow future teams to pick up the work with recommended next steps linked to clear user-led evidence.

2

Recommend an approach for implementing the prioritised features

View details
  • Recommend options for implementing prioritised features informed by research, design and tech findings.
  • Provide the programme with an understanding of where process gaps exist and what steps will need to be taken in order to implement prioritised features, including any legal, cultural and governance challenges.

3

Knowledge sharing across teams within the wider programme

View details

Collaborate with teams to ensure outputs are targeted, digestible and ready to handover to the teams likely to implement the features.

What's not in the Phase 3 scope
  • Strategies for onboarding services - the roadmap for onboarding services to cross government features is complicated and best sits within the accounts/onboarding team. X-Gov Services sits within the account rather than being a separate product.
  • Research with services or end users
    • services have a lack of readiness to engage in technical conversations
    • avoiding duplication - phase 2 research generated a lot of insights from end users and services, which are not fully incorporated into the hub
