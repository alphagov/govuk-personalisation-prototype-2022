The goal of our project is to understand end user and service needs and expectations of a seamless, joined up and personalised experience for users when completing sequential tasks across more than one government service.

Life while having a baby

Phase 1 focussed on joining up services within a predictable, linear user journey.

Stages of life often happen simultaneously, for example, buying a larger home or needing to learn how to drive because you’re expecting a baby.

Therefore, phase 2 focused on cutting across multiple user journeys, allowing us to understand and test the complex scenarios and challenges of joining up services across government.

Life event criteria
  • A wide range of services and departments across government
  • Includes users that are easy to reach for user research
  • A diverse range of service users
  • Service owners that are easy to access and engage with
  • Has data that we are able to access
  • Supports the features we want to test
  • Include a broad set of data
  • Spans a couple of years in a person’s life

Meet Penny

Penny and her husband got married last year and are soon to be expecting a baby. It is their first child so they’re not sure of the order in which they need to put things in place and what they are eligible for.

They currently live in the city but have decided that it is too hectic for a life with a newborn. They are ready to buy their first home together and have decided to do so outside of the city. Penny also realises that this means she will need to learn how to drive as amenities will no longer be on her doorstep like they are now in the city.

Moving to a new area means she will have to quit her job, and she isn’t sure when she will be going back to work after their baby is born so she needs to find out what support she and her husband can get throughout that period.
