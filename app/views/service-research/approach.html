In the second phase of the project, we wanted to broaden our research to include government services.

Our main focus was to explore the feasibility of joining up services across government, whilst identifying what service features would help make the GOV.UK account more personalised.

For the research, we wanted to represent the needs and views of a diverse range of government service teams.

We did this by broadening the recruitment profile beyond the services within the life event (‘Life while having a baby’) to include a range of central government services and small local authorities.

Objectives

  1. Determine the complexity vs. value of different account features identified in the backlog
  2. Capture the risks, dependencies and complexities of joining up services

What we wanted to find out

  • Understand the service expectations and challenges for each feature
  • How each of the features could deliver future value to a service
  • Results from the sessions aggregated into a matrix, showing feature placement across value and complexity of adoption

Methodology

We did an interview and moderated card sorting with service owners and product managers.

Any features associated with account setup were excluded from the initial service research. We focused on prioritising features that could be enabled after the account has the capability to save and reuse data.

We discussed each of these 13 hypothetical account features and asked participants to card sort each feature in 2 ways:

  • the perceived potential future value to the service
  • the perceived complexity of adoption

Participants

  1. MOJ - Lasting power of attorney
  2. DEFRA - Future Farming
  3. HMRC - Register a trust
  4. Camden LA - The Camden account
  5. Northampton Council - Blue Badge
  6. DWP - Personal independence payment (PIP)

Features included in the research

  • Verified data
  • Global push
  • Notifications
  • Reminders
  • Use of existing data
  • Fixed vs editable data
  • Share specific data with another person’s GOV.UK account
  • Saved documents
  • Suggested eligibility
  • Calculated data based on known information (Derived data)
  • Personalised content
  • Autofill forms
  • Prompt user to complete profile

Due to limited availability at the end of the year, many service teams were unable to support with user research.
