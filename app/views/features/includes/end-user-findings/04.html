User reactions to curated content were fairly unremarkable.

Throughout research sessions, we had to draw the participant’s attention to curated content so that we could engage them in discussion about the concept.

Users were open to curated content in our concept test, saying that they understood they had consented (fictional consent) to the information being used as part of their GOV.UK account.

Some users were uncomfortable about their child’s information being displayed in-page. Although this work did not uncover any common pieces of data that were too intrusive to use as part of curated content, this should be considered as the programme of work progresses.

We acknowledge that across a diverse user audience, different people will have different tipping points around what they perceive as intrusive.

We observed users scanning the page for relevant information and viewing elements of in-page curation as accurate to them. If users believe that curated content is accurate, particularly on eligibility pages, then this could create a liability risk. Read more on this insight
