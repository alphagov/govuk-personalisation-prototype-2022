User research participants were served links of things they may be interested in based on their past activity (N.B. this was based on an imaginary scenario, not a real-life one).

User reactions to service recommendations were neutral and unremarkable. Some users did not notice the service recommendations until we drew their attention to them.

Users understood why they were seeing the service recommendations.

Service recommendations as a concept was outshone by Suggested eligibility, which was tested in the same round.


A note on user research methodology - we cannot evaluate whether or not users would click on suggested links in user research sessions, because the session is based on a hypothetical scenario where there is no genuine task or transaction.

Suggested links could be part of a front-end multi-variate test, however, we would strongly recommend collaboration with legal and policy.

An initial conversation with the legal team suggested that there should be further consideration on the ethics of how links are served - that is, if users miss important information because the service they require is pushed further down a list or page, there could be some liability issues.
