Reminders were understood and well received.

Users have varied preferences around medium; some prefer SMS, others prefer email. Many users were familiar with ‘add to calendar’. No one was familiar with the idea of ‘subscribing’ to a calendar.

Users feel that reminders should be sent in advance of an event for important events or actions.

One user spoke of their experience with mental health and said that they rely on reminders to get things done, and it helps with their anxiety.

Given the variation of preferences, users should be given the option to control and manage reminder options.

Examples from user research:

“It's very good - we may not remember we need to do these things within a time frame. That calendar reminder is one of the best things!”

“I do like to receive things in advance. I suffer from anxiety, so planning things in advance is a big thing for me.”

from phase 2, round 3
