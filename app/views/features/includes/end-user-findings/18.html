Throughout user research we heard that 'having everything in one place' was useful and valuable.

We tested the concept of adding documents to the GOV.UK account which was met with mixed responses. Whilst some users saw this as useful, others saw it as a potential risk if their account was ever compromised.

It is important to note here that user research is not real life - whilst some users were open to the concept of uploading documents, evaluating this in the safety of the research session is not the same as doing this in real life.

Users expected an area for saved documents could be used in a variety of ways:

  • a way of proving ID/sharing ID
  • a place to save the last three years' residential addresses
  • official documents that could 'evidence' a claim
  • As a space to upload letters with reference numbers e.g. hospital letters

One user envisaged a future scenario where documents automatically appear in this section rather than the user having to upload them.

“Good to have everything in one place really. I think it’s handy to have all your documents and things like that because there’s a lot of people who carry stuff around. Like I’ve been in a panic whenever I’ve took my passport out thinking I’m gonna lose it. So if you could get it digitally on a system where like say if I ever needed a DBS check or whatever I could retrieve it from there, that would make me feel a lot less stressed about the process. I like it, I think it’s a good place to have everything, And because it’s an official website as well. A lot of the time you’ve got to send off things haven’t you, like for example if you have a child, you have to send off your child’s birth certificate to claim the child benefit. Whereas if it was an uploaded document onto there then that would cut out a lot of the sending off. Because that’s always a panic if you’re sending off a passport, or if you’re renewing a passport or anything like that, it’s always quite a stressful process I think. Whereas if it was online and you could see the progress of your application like whether it’s been received or anything like that, then that would be a lot easier than you know just kind of waiting around for it to come in the post. Cos it just gets delivered randomly doesn’t it. When you get a passport you don’t get any - it’s not like you can track it or anything like that which is sometimes a bit stressful. So I don’t know, I think it’s a good concept really.”
