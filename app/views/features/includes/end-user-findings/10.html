Throughout the user research sessions, users were able to easily navigate to the account, and subsequently to settings. Users were able to toggle on and off to configure the settings.

We received a variety of views around how users would interact with these settings; some commented that they would switch them all on, others had certain preferences for configuration.

The concept of settings was understood, however, there were some elements of content that were unclear to some users.

Examples from user research:

"Pretty straightforward, easy to change, turn on and off" phase 2, round 2, participant 3

"I don't need recommendations. Would it inundate me?" phase 2, round 1, participant 3

“I don’t understand what ‘make dates, locations and amounts relevant to me’ means” phase 2, round 5, participant 3

"I'd also want to see something - a policy - on how my information is used and how it's shared. A breakdown on how it's used, won't be shared with third parties...you want your information to be protected." phase 2, round 1, participant 4

Further content exploration around the management of settings would be needed as the programme of work progresses.
