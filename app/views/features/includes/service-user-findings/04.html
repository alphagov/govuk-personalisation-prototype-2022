Generally service owners saw this feature as a nice to have - but not a feature that would make or break a service. For some services they struggled to attribute a value to personalising the experience.

A good number of services mentioned they felt that personalisation was not something that works for government in the same way it would for Amazon or the BBC, for example. The overwhelming view was that service users are coming to government to do a specific thing.

Personalisation was seen as helpful in particular scenarios - but to do this well, services felt that there was a high level of complexity and work involved.

"My only consideration would be the level of confidence we've got about the user being the person sitting at the keyboard, we do need to be really confident in our information." [HMRC]
