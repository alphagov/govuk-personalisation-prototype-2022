This feature was seen as having the potential to be really useful for services across a wide range of use cases:

"Really valuable, anything we can do to tell the user what they are eligible or entitled for is really good." [PIP]

However, there was considerable concern from services about user adoption/reactions. In particular, finding how to strike the right balance between government knowing everything about the user while also helping the user:

"For users who don't understand what services are out there and can help them, but from a privacy activist perspective you'll get a lot of push back. Not from individuals so much as organisations acting on individuals behalf." [MOJ]

Services have concerns that suggesting eligibility will increase the volumes of enquiries they receive from users. Therefore, careful consideration is needed around how this feature works:

"Suggested eligibility based on new grants at a global level poses a risk to services." [West Northamptonshire Council - Blue Badge]

Service use case

  • A lack of awareness is a key issue for PIP. If someone is applying for a benefit as they have a long term condition or severe disability - if they are getting Universal Credit because they have been deemed not fit for work, that should give us a good indicator [of eligibility for PIP). If they apply for another benefit which isn't PIP - it makes them aware of PIP." DWP
  • "There is a lack of understanding and lots of people that applied for PIP already and might want to re-apply" DWP
  • "Any exemption form has to be manually checked at the moment, it would be a significant return on investment from a caseworker perspective [if eligibility was known]." MOJ
  • "We've got 1 really good use case - LPA120 anyone receving Universal Credit/benefits can get a free subsidised LPA and at the moment you have to prove it with a letter, and if we could offer it without you even having to be aware you're eligible it would be really great." MOJ
