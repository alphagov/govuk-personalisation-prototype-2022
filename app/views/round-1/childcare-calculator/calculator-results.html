Free childcare hours

You could get up to 30 hours free childcare a week - this is 1,140 hours a year.

You can get free hours for your 3 or 4 year old that can be used in or outside of school term time. Your provider may apply charges for additional services.

How free childcare hours works

You can take up the offer for the school term following your child’s third birthday. For example, if they turn 3 on 18 October, you will be able to get free childcare from January.

You must use childcare from an approved provider. For example:

  • a registered childminder
  • playscheme
  • nursery
  • out of school club
  • home careworker working for a registered agency

You may be able to use your free hours between 2 different providers.

You can contact your childcare provider or your local council to find out more about free childcare hours (opens in new tab).

Free childcare hours stop when your child starts in reception class.


Tax-Free Childcare

You could get £3,120 a year towards your childcare costs.

You can get up to £2,000 a year towards your childcare costs for each child aged 11 or under, or up to £4,000 a year for each child aged 16 or under if they are disabled. You get a £2 government top-up for every £8 you pay in childcare.

How Tax-Free Childcare works

You will create an online childcare account to pay into, get top-ups paid into it and use it to pay your approved provider directly. For example, for every £8 paid into the account, the government will pay a £2 top-up that would give you £10 towards your childcare costs for each child.

You can use it to pay for up to £10,000 of childcare for each child a year – so you’d get up to £2,000 in top-ups a year, or £4,000 a year if they are disabled.

The child or children must usually live with you. They stop being eligible on 1 September after their 11th birthday.


Universal Credit

You may be able to claim back up to 85% of your childcare costs if you’re eligible for Universal Credit.
