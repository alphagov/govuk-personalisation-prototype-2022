We conducted user research with citizens to understand the perceived benefits and drawbacks of the GOV.UK account within joined up government transactions.

{% endblock %} {% else %} {% endif %} {% if pagemenu[1] %} {% block section2 %}

In order to explore this concept we created a variety of scenarios around the life event of ‘Life while having a baby’.

We used front end prototypes with user research participants, primarily to elicit discussions around the concept and the features presented.

We were not focussing on usability testing, we anticipate that will follow at a future point in the programme of work.

We spoke to 52 user research participants over the course of 9 rounds of research from August 2021 - December 2021.

For the majority of participants we engaged with, the concept of the GOV.UK account within joined up transactions across government was well received.

  1. Users identified the benefits of the concept of the GOV.UK account within cross gov transactions as:
    • Everything in one place
    • Time saving
    • A consistent, easy and efficient way of dealing with the government
    • One login for everything
  2. Not everyone will perceive the GOV.UK account as valuable or useful
    • Some users questioned its usefulness and commented they would only use it if there was a clear reason for doing so
    • Some users are sceptical about entering data into an online service.

    3. N.B. This work did not seek to predict or quantify the segment of the future audience who would be pro / con GOV.UK accounts. It would not be good practice to do so since this work is still hypothetical.

  3. Users want to be clear on how their data is being used.
  4. User controls and functions for the management of their personal data were received positively in user research.
  5. Users expect their data to be processed [if they enter it into a GOV.UK account]
  6. Users see personalised or curated content as being accurate for them.
  7. In their own words, users describe the GOV.UK account as: hub, umbrella, one stop shop, all under one roof
  8. Users don’t care how their data is stored, as long as it is secure.
  9. Users expect the government’s online services to be safe and secure.
