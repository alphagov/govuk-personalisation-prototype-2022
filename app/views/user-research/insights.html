A recurring piece of feedback was the usefulness of having everything in one place.

Users also commented on:

  • Time saving
  • A consistent, easy and efficient way of dealing with the government
  • One login for everything

N.B. we were testing a concept that we had designed for user research, the GOV.UK account does not exist with such a full range of functionality in real life.

Generally speaking, users reacted positively to the concept of the GOV.UK account as part of a x-gov transaction, however, there were various comments around its usefulness being dependent on whether or not there was a genuine reason to use it.

Comments in user research highlighted the fact that throughout certain periods of life (i.e. childcare) the likelihood of interacting with government services is much higher.

For some, depending on lifestyle, the regularity of interactions with the government is much lower. Some users clearly articulated that they would find the account useful ‘now’ (during childcare) whereas otherwise they may not see the point.

Further work around profile raising and adoption of the account will likely be needed in the future to understand how best to compel users to create an account and engage with its features.

There will be some data sceptics within the future audience who will be unlikely to save data to a GOV.UK account. This project does not seek to quantify or predict this potential segment.
